# OPT-274: Tighten Rule 1 group routing: require chain overlap with open lots

## Bug

Two independent positions opened by separate broker orders at the same `(account, underlying, expiration)` get merged into one group, even though they're on distinct `chain_id`s — i.e., they are independent positions.

[OPT-270](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-270/roll-chain-breaks-when-two-independent-positions-on-the-same) fixed the *closed-anchor* version of this (a closed lot's expiration was pulling unrelated new opens into the wrong group). But Rule 1 still triggers when both candidates are open: the OPEN-check passes, the chains aren't checked, and the new lot joins.

## Fix

In `assign_lots_to_groups` (pure) and `GroupPersister.process_groups` (DB persistence), tighten Rule 1 to also require that at least one **open** lot in the candidate group share the new lot's `chain_id`. If no chain match, fall through to Rule 3 (create a new group).

This is a one-line tightening on top of [OPT-270](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-270/roll-chain-breaks-when-two-independent-positions-on-the-same)'s existing `_is_group_open(gk)` guard.

## Test

Already in place: `tests/unit/test_orchestrator.py::TestRollMechanics::test_separate_opens_at_same_strike_and_expiration_create_separate_groups` is xfail strict. When this fix lands, it flips to xpass and the marker comes off.
