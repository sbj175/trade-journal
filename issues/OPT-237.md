# OPT-237: Transactional email service

A transactional email service is infrastructure for sending \*\*automated, triggered emails\*\* — as opposed to marketing/outreach emails or personal correspondence.

\*\*Examples of transactional emails:\*\*

\- "Confirm your email address" on signup

\- "Your password reset link"

\- "Your tastytrade sync completed"

\- "Your trial expires in 3 days"

\- Invoice/receipt emails

\*\*Why not just use Gmail/Workspace for this?\*\*

Your app's backend (running on Railway) needs to programmatically send emails via API or SMTP. Gmail accounts aren't designed for that — they have low limits, require OAuth gymnastics, and aren't reliable at scale. Transactional services give you a simple API call from your code and handle all the delivery infrastructure.

\*\*The main players:\*\*

\- \*\*Resend\*\* — newest, developer-friendly, generous free tier (3,000 emails/month), very clean API. Popular with indie SaaS builders right now

\- \*\*Postmark\*\* — excellent deliverability reputation, slightly more expensive

\- \*\*SendGrid\*\* — ubiquitous but bloated; owned by Twilio

\- \*\*AWS SES\*\* — dirt cheap but more setup work

\*\*For OptionLedger specifically\*\*, Resend is probably the right call. It's what most solo devs are using today, the free tier covers you through beta easily, and it integrates cleanly with Node/Python backends. You'd send from something like \`noreply@optionledger.app\` and could also route \`support@optionledger.app\` replies there.

It's a pretty quick integration — an afternoon of work at most.
