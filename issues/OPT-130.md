# OPT-130: Email campaign manager


## Comments

Since you’re building **OptionLedger**, I’ve condensed this guide to help you scale from a "solo builder MVP" to a "real SaaS with automation" without the excessive spacing.

---

### 🟢 Easiest + Fastest (Recommended for Early Stage)

**Stack:** Your App Backend + Resend/SendGrid (Transactional) + ConvertKit/Mailchimp (Automation).

**Flow:**

1. User signs up in OptionLedger.
2. Your backend creates the user in your DB and adds them to your marketing tool via API.
3. The marketing tool handles the automated drip sequence, unsubscribes, and CAN-SPAM compliance.

   **Why this works:** No cron jobs or queue management; you get a visual automation builder and maximum marketing flexibility.

### 🟡 Developer-Controlled (Custom Logic)

**Stack:** Your Backend (Node/Django/etc.) + Postgres + Resend/Amazon SES + Background Jobs (BullMQ/Celery).

**Flow:**

1. User signs up; you insert records into `users` and `email_sequence_progress` tables.
2. A daily scheduled job finds users due for the next email, sends it via API, and updates `next_send_at`.

   **Why this works:** Fully customizable and no monthly marketing fees. Best if you want to trigger emails based on specific app behavior (e.g., "User logged 3 trades but hasn't journaled yet").

### 🔵 The Hybrid Approach (Highly Recommended)

Keep marketing logic **out** of your core app. Use **Resend** for transactional emails (password resets, receipts) and **ConvertKit** for onboarding drips. This keeps your codebase clean and prevents you from accidentally building an entire email marketing platform yourself.

### ⚙️ Technical Essentials

Regardless of your choice, do not skip these for **optionledger.app**:

* **Authentication:** Set up SPF, DKIM, and DMARC immediately to ensure deliverability.
* **Subdomain:** Consider using a subdomain like `mail.optionledger.app` for your sending identity.
* **Warm-up:** Start sending slowly to build your domain reputation with inbox providers.

### 💰 Strategy for Your Current Stage

1. **Phase 1 (MVP):** Use ConvertKit for drips and Resend for transactional. Focus on the product.
2. **Phase 2 (Revenue):** Move complex behavioral logic in-house if specific triggers are needed.
3. **Phase 3 (Scale):** Implement full segmentation based on trading styles (e.g., scalpers vs. swing traders).
