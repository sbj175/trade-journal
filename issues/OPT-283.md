# OPT-283: Spec amendment: broker order/chain not authoritative for rolls (§0.1, §0.2)

## What landed

Doc-only spec amendment in `docs/roll-detection-spec.md` (commit `601c6ce`):

* **§0.1** Broker order/ticket structure is not authoritative — neither `order_id` nor `chain_id` (which Stage 3 propagates from ROLLING tickets) can be the deciding signal for whether something is a roll. The structural truth is same-day, lot-level pairing of compatible closes and opens.
* **§0.2** A lot is in at most one chain. Quantity conservation at branch points: the smaller of (closed-quantity, opened-quantity) is paired; excess on either side is independent.
* §1.4 (chain overlap) and §1.5 (signature match) annotated with the gap that the in-place fix would have closed.

## Scope change

The original ticket also proposed an in-place patch to `_detect_roll_links` to fix the IBIT 42C "roll + add" bug. That patch is **not landing** — during design discussion the user committed to a deeper restructure (lot-level lineage with `parent_lot_id` as the source of truth, position-level lineage as a derived view). The IBIT bug is fixed *by* that migration, not before it.

The bug fix moves to **OPT-284** (the migration epic). This ticket is closed once the spec amendment is on main.

## Why a deeper restructure

Group-level chains can branch (one source, multiple children), and any branching at the chain level forces a parent's realized P&L to appear in multiple children's `cumulative_realized_pnl` — total portfolio P&L stops being the sum of chain P&Ls. Strict additivity ("the math works") requires that each chain element have at most one parent, which is naturally true at the lot level but not at the group level. Lot-level chains are linear by construction; group-level chains can't be without losing trader intent.

See OPT-284 for the migration plan.
