# OPT-276: Iron Condor strike label drops legs that closed on earlier days

## Bug

A 4-leg Iron Condor whose put wing was closed on one day and call wing on a later day rendered as `Iron Condor 310/320` instead of `Iron Condor 200/210/310/320`. The put-side strikes vanished from the strategy label even though all four legs were held to exit.

Real example: TSLA 200P/210P/310C/320C Iron Condor opened 2025-04-15. Put wing closed 2025-05-09; call wing closed 2025-05-13. Group label showed `310/320` only.

## Root cause

`src/services/roll_timeline.py::_current_strike_label` filtered the "final cohort" of a closed group to only lots whose closings landed on the same calendar day as the group's latest close (`max_close_day`). The same-day tolerance was meant to handle "long and short sides filled a couple minutes apart" — it doesn't account for traders unwinding wings on different days.

## Fix

Replace the close-date heuristic with the structural truth from the roll-event walk: a lot is *not* part of the final position only if its `lot_id` appears as a `closed.lot_id` in some `roll_event`'s `pairs[]` (i.e., its close was paired with a new opening on the same day, indicating it was rolled out). All other lots — including ones closed on earlier days during a wing-by-wing exit — are part of the final structure and contribute to the strike label.

`compute_roll_timeline` already computes `roll_events` before calling `_current_strike_label`. Now passes them in.
