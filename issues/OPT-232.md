# OPT-232: Tastytrade trusted partner process

That's a great sign — Peter's tone is casual and encouraging, and "simple KYC" for a read-only app tracks exactly with what you'd expect. They're not going to scrutinize you like a broker-dealer; they just need to confirm you're a legitimate person building a legitimate thing.

Here's what I'd expect they're actually looking for:

\*\*Identity/legitimacy signals\*\*

\- You're a real person with a real business (Keystone Drone Solutions LLC or OptionLedger as a DBA works)

\- A professional-looking web presence — even a landing page is sufficient

\- No red flags (scammy aesthetics, data harvesting vibes, shady use case)

\*\*Application purpose clarity\*\*

\- That OptionLedger is a journaling/analytics tool, NOT executing trades on users' behalf

\- That the OAuth scope is read-only (positions, order history, account data)

\- That user data stays private and isn't being resold or aggregated commercially in a sketchy way

\*\*What they'll actually review\*\*

\- Your landing page — so it should look credible and clearly describe what the app does

\- The demo/video — showing the sync flow, what data is pulled, how it's displayed

\- Possibly a privacy policy link

Since Peter called it "simple KYC," I'd interpret that as: \*is this a real app built by a real person for a legitimate purpose, or is it someone trying to scrape TT user data?\* You clear that bar easily.

\*\*Your biggest asset here is the landing page.\*\* If it currently looks rough, that's worth a quick polish pass before you send the demo. The video doesn't need to be produced — a Loom walkthrough of the OAuth flow and the Positions/Reports pages would be totally sufficient.
