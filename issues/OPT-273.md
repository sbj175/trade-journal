# OPT-273: Same-direction multi-leg roll collapses parallel chains in position_ledger

## Bug

`src/pipeline/position_ledger.py` \~L134-141: when a ROLLING order touches lots from multiple chain_ids (parallel ladder rungs being rolled together in one broker order), the code picks one chain arbitrarily via `next(iter(affected_chains))` and assigns it to every new opening lot. The other chains are silently dropped, collapsing parallel positions into a single chain.

Discovered while testing [OPT-270](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-270/roll-chain-breaks-when-two-independent-positions-on-the-same). The IBIT covered-call ladder showed inflated chain lengths (12+ rolls reported for an 8-roll history) because historical multi-rung rolls had collapsed chains and the BFS in `roll_chain_summary` walked the merged graph.

## Companion to [OPT-262](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-262/roll-detection-misses-puts-only-roll-on-multi-leg-iron-condor)

[OPT-262](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-262/roll-detection-misses-puts-only-roll-on-multi-leg-iron-condor) fixed the *splitter* side — it correctly keeps multi-leg roll orders intact when closes and opens mirror each other in (option_type, direction). But the `position_ledger` side that consumes those intact orders never properly paired closes with opens; it just picks one chain for everyone. So [OPT-262](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-262/roll-detection-misses-puts-only-roll-on-multi-leg-iron-condor) + this fix together complete the multi-leg roll story.

## Fix

Replace the `next(iter(affected_chains))` broadcast with quantity-aware FIFO pairing:

1. Walk `order.closing_transactions` in order. For each close, peek at `lot_manager.get_open_lots(symbol)` (already FIFO) and build a queue of `(chain_id, qty_taken)` entries totaling the close's quantity.
2. For each opening transaction, pop chains from the queue head until its quantity is satisfied. The head chain becomes that lot's `chain_id`.
3. Replace the broadcast `temp_chain_id` with a per-opening-tx lookup in the resulting map.

## Edge cases

* N closes ↔ N opens, equal quantities → trivial 1:1 pairing (covers IBIT case)
* One close of size 40 spanning two 20-contract chains → queue captures both entries from that one close
* Asymmetric counts (M closes ≠ K opens) → fall back to current "broadcast first chain" behavior, log a warning
