# OPT-251: Landing: add real product screenshots with descriptive alt text

The landing page has zero product imagery. Visitors land on pure copy with no visual proof that the product exists. This hurts conversion AND SEO (images with alt text are indexed for Google Images + add keyword density).

## Plan

Capture high-quality screenshots from the running app and place them in `landing/screenshots/`:

1. **Hero visual** — Positions page showing an Iron Condor with live P&L, Greeks, DTE. Place below the hero headline or as a grid item. (Biggest conversion impact.)
2. **Roll chain** — Ledger page or RollChainModal showing a 3-roll chain with aggregate P&L across rolls. Place in Features section next to "Real P&L Across Rolls". (Differentiator vs competitors.)
3. **Strategy recognition** — Positions list showing auto-detected Iron Condors, Verticals, Covered Calls. Place next to "Strategy Recognition" feature.
4. **Reports dashboard** — Performance chart from Reports page. Place in a new "Analyze your edge" section or near features.

## Alt-text pattern

Every image needs descriptive alt text that includes a keyword:

* `alt="Tastytrade Iron Condor position with live P&L and Greeks in OptionLedger"`
* `alt="Three-roll chain on a short put with aggregate profit/loss"`
* NOT `alt="screenshot"` or `alt="dashboard"`

## Technical

* Export at 2x retina (e.g. 1600px wide) then serve at 800px with `srcset`
* Use WebP with PNG fallback
* Add explicit `width`/`height` attributes to prevent CLS
* Lazy-load images below the fold (`loading="lazy"`)

## Depends on

Nothing. Can ship independently, though best done after [OPT-247](https://linear.app/optionedge/issue/OPT-247/landing-precompile-tailwind-instead-of-cdn) (Tailwind precompile) so the page is already fast.

## Why

* **Conversion**: "Show don't tell" — visitors can't trust product claims without seeing the UI.
* **SEO**: Google Images traffic is real (especially for query "tastytrade journal screenshot"). Descriptive filenames and alt text both contribute.
* **Social**: Screenshot-heavy pages get more Twitter/Reddit shares than copy-only pages.
